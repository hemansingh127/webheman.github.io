Hemansingh Lutchmun is a Mauritian-born artist based and works between Frankfurt Main, Germany and Goodlands, Mauritius. His practice spans film, sculpture, and installation. Interweaving raw materials such as clay, soil and sand, with time-based techniques, his work navigates the intersections of labor, extraction, and displacement. Lutchmunâ€™s practice weaves together personal experiences and broader cultural symbols to examine memory, transformation and enticement.

Expanding on these themes, Lutchmun explores how global processes shape the personal feelings of identity and belonging. At the heart of his practice is a contemplation of the fluid boundaries between the personal, the spiritual and the communal, and how those are transformed through labour into tangible symbols and persisting narratives.
